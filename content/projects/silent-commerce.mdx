## Brief

Many small-business websites fail in the same places: unclear first screen, weak navigation, slow pages, buried contact details, and inconsistent visual rhythm. This case-study format shows how a redesign can make the business easier to understand and easier to contact.

## Approach

The work starts with a practical audit: what users need, what the current site hides, and where the design loses trust. From there, the front end is rebuilt around simple content sections, direct calls to action, responsive layout, and a visual system that feels considered without becoming decorative.

## Outcome

The goal is a site that loads quickly, answers obvious questions, and turns interest into contact. The same structure can support restaurants, consultants, studios, local services, and independent operators.
