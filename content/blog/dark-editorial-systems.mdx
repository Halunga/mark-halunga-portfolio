## A Personal Site Is A Thinking Surface

A mature personal platform should hold more than a biography and a grid of thumbnails. It should make space for unfinished thoughts, serious images, older work, field notes, and the kind of research that becomes useful only after it has been revisited.

That is why the system matters. The site needs a place for polished project pages, but it also needs a place for fragments that deserve a future.

## Darkness As Editorial Pressure

A dark interface can quickly become theatrical. The goal is not mood for its own sake. Darkness works when it gives the image more pressure, the text more concentration, and the page more quiet.

The palette here stays narrow: coal background, off-white text, muted secondary copy, and one restrained accent. The drama comes from scale and pacing, not decoration.

## The Replaceable Machine

The best version of a portfolio is easy to edit. Projects live in content files. Blog posts live in MDX. Site settings and navigation live in small data modules. The visual system is expressive, but the maintenance system is plain.

That combination lets the platform keep changing without needing to be redesigned every time the work grows.
